🎬 Core Concept

"How do we provide world-class hospitality to our customers through our product development process?"

Like a Michelin-star restaurant, every detail matters. The PDLC is our "mise en place" - intentional preparation that replaces chaos with confidence.

💡 Key Shift: From "solution-first impulse" → "learning-driven approach"

🔄 The 4 Phases

🔍 Phase 1: Discovery

Goal: Know enough to define the problem (not perfect documentation)

"What problem are we actually solving?"

The Bear Analogy: Carmy can't fix the kitchen until he understands the "ingredients" and assigns roles based on strengths.

Activities Checklist:

  • Define the problem (resist jumping to solutions)
  • Research existing patterns/approaches
  • Align on what we're solving
  • Create living templates (iterate as we learn)

Decision Gate: Do we have enough signal to move forward?

✏️ Phase 2: Define

Goal: Translate insights into rough wireframes + actionable requirements

"How will our solution actually meet the needs we identified?"

The Bear Analogy: "The Forks" episode - Every detail down to the smudge on a fork is a defined requirement of 5-star service.

Activities Checklist:

  • Create wireframes (rough, intentional)
  • Document requirements (clinical + user needs)
  • Align cross-functional teams (Product, Design, Clinical, Engineering)
  • Ensure every detail has purpose

Decision Gate: Does the solution concept meet our defined needs?

🎯 Phase 3: Plan

Goal: Resolve ALL unknowns before Engineering starts building

"Are we ready to 'let it rip'?"

The Bear Analogy: Under the table scene - Carmy and Sydney fixing the bolt together, ensuring foundation is solid before doors open.

Activities Checklist:

  • Finalize requirements (no more swirl)
  • Create release plan
  • Confirm every team says "heard!"
  • Shift from "I think we're ready" → "I know we're ready"
💡 This is your "mise en place" - everything in its place before service starts

Decision Gate: Is Engineering ready to build with confidence?

🚀 Phase 4: Build & Validate

Goal: Feature that actually works for customers + rapid support

"Does the guest leave satisfied?"

The Bear Analogy: Friends & Family night - Execute under pressure, pivot when the fridge breaks, ensure validated success.

Activities Checklist:

  • Execute the build efficiently
  • Learn and adjust in real-time (like a tasting spoon)
  • Handle post-launch issues quickly ("re-fire the dish")
  • User validation (not just shipping code)
  • Relentless improvement

Success = Guest satisfaction, not just sending the plate out

📝 Video Summaries

Quick reference summaries of all training videos - Use these to review key points without rewatching.

Introduction to PDLC

4 Phases Overview:

  • Phase 1 (Discovery): Align on problem → Validated problem definition
  • Phase 2 (Define): Wireframes, requirements, designs → Clear actionable requirements
  • Phase 3 (Plan): Confirm readiness to build → Build-ready plan with completed artifacts
  • Phase 4 (Build & Validate): Execute efficiently → Successful feature launch validated by users

Notable Changes from Current Process:

  • Earlier Engineering involvement: Mapping process prior to design
  • Focus on LoFi wireframes: Before design creation

Key Takeaway:

Process is iterative: Move fast - but know when to slow down

Phase 1 Overview - Activities & Descriptions

Activities:

  • 1.01 Context, Problem & Goal Definition: Problem Definition (Product owns)
  • 1.02 ClinSol Insights: ClinSol Insights (ClinSol owns)
  • 1.03 Foundational UXR: UXR Research Doc (UXR owns)
  • 1.04 Competitor Reference: Competitive Analysis (Product owns)
  • 1.05 Domain Modeling Draft: Domain Model Draft (Design owns)
  • 1.06 Technical Feasibility & Analysis: Technical Feasibility Doc (Engineering owns)
Phase 1 Gantt - Decision Making

Key Question:

"Do we have confidence that we're solving the right problem for the right users?"

Exit Criteria:

  • Validated problem definition with key stakeholders
  • Understand user needs and pain points from UXR
  • Identified competitive landscape and differentiation
  • Technical feasibility confirmed (no major blockers)
  • Team alignment on problem space and potential solution directions
Phase 2 Overview - Activities & Descriptions

Activities:

  • 2.01 Requirements Draft & Wireframe Creation: Wireframes Document (Design owns)
  • 2.02 Design Creation + Requirements Refined: Requirements Document (Product owns)
  • 2.02 Design Creation + Requirements Refined: Design Document (Design owns)
Phase 2 Gantt - Decision Making

Key Question:

"Do we collectively feel comfortable with the direction and level of detail in the draft requirements and initial designs?"

Important Principles:

  • NOT a "check the box" exercise
  • NOT based on artifact perfection
  • Can always update prior phase artifacts with new information
  • Requirements/designs will be completed in Phase 3

Gantt Chart Insights:

  • Activities happen in parallel (drafting requirements + wireframes simultaneously)
  • Phase 1 artifacts (process maps, ClinSol insights) were revisited and updated
  • Requirements can inform wireframing and vice versa

Outcome: Actionable requirements and early designs that translate discovery insights into a firmer solution concept

Phase 3 Overview - Activities & Descriptions

Sub-phase 3.01 - Detailed Understanding:

  • Complete Requirements (Design owns)
  • Complete Designs (Design owns)
  • Engineering Estimate "Timeline Range" (Engineering owns)

Sub-phase 3.02 - Release Planning:

  • Release Plan Development (CM owns)
Phase 3 Gantt - Decision Making

Key Question:

"Do we understand the solution & risks well enough to begin in-depth development?"

Exit Criteria:

  • Artifacts sufficiently documented and reviewed by all Pod members
  • Technical Lead comfortable with remaining open questions
  • Team agrees: Cannot change requirements/designs after Phase 4 starts
  • Engineering decides acceptable change threshold (with Product + DM input)
  • Team agrees to move forward (perfection not required)

Key Principle: We've learned enough to responsibly move forward (not "phase is done")

Phase 4 Overview - Activities & Descriptions

Sub-phase 4.01 - Product Build & Demos:

  • Build the Product Features (Engineering owns)
  • Demo Feature Sets throughout Build Phase (Engineering owns)
  • Engineering Estimate: "Delivery Date" (Engineering owns)
  • Release Plan Refinement (DM owns)

Sub-phase 4.02 - Testing:

  • Complete all Required QA Testing (QA owns)
  • Conduct PDAT Testing (DM owns)

Other Sub-phases:

  • 4.03 Customer UAT
  • 4.04 Training
  • 4.05 Product Feature Launch

Checklist Item:

  • Complete Go/No Go Decisioning: DM-led cross-functional review (product readiness, technical stability)
Phase 4 Gantt - Post-Launch Decision

Gantt Timeline (Days 21-65):

Shows Phase 3 and Phase 4 activities running in parallel:

  • Phase 3 (Green): Days 21-30 (Requirements, Designs, Estimates, Release Plan)
  • Phase 4 (Purple): Days 30-65+ (Build, Demos, QA, PDAT, UAT, Training, Launch, Hypercare)

Key Question (Post-Launch):

"Do we feel comfortable with the number & severity of issues reported during Post-Launch Hypercare, so we can end Hypercare by a specific date?"

Exit Criteria:

  • Review Hypercare issues and decide if can close Release
  • Account for timeline to implement fixes
  • Transition to standard "Support" model

Outcome: Successful feature launch validated by users, supported by stable performance and rapid post-launch issue resolution
